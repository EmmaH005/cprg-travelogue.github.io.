Muiderslot is located in Muiden, a city in the Netherlands. The castle itself holds a lot of fascinating history and artwork. Some of its most unique features include original furnishings, a garden maintained with traditional herbs and plants, and its battle history. For someone who wants to feel as though they've been transported back in time, this castle will provide the desired effect. Some of my most treasured photos originated from Muiderslot.

Fun Fact:

Muiderslot's residents slept upright in belief it was unhealthy to lay down for long periods of time.

Giethoorn is a small canal village in the Netherlands. One of its main attractions is canal and boat tours that navigate Giethoorn and the surrounding body of water. Here, you can sit on waterside patios and enjoy the calm surrounding enviornment. This town boasts lush vegetation and cobble sidewalks, making for an authentic european experience. If you find yourself walking its streets, stop by the various shops, or take a seat on a bench and chat with the many ducks that populate the canals.

Fun Fact:

The name Giethoorn originated from goat horns discovered during its founding.
The Magna Plaza, Located in Amsterdam, is a architectural masterpiece. This building is built in a neo-gothic fashion, and is a valuable location to Amsterdam. In plain terms, its a shopping center. A better description however, is a place to discover Dutch heritage and tradition while exporing the intricate details of a beautiful building. This is a great location to go with others, take photos, grab a bite, and shop, of course!


Fun Fact:

"Shopping Cart" translated to Dutch is "Winkelwagen" [vink-el-vag-en]
